Saba Budaya Baduy

Source: Courtesy of Google and Wikimedia

Saba Budaya Baduy, also known as Baduy Community, is a traditional community living in the southwestern part of the Indonesian province of Banten, on the island of Java. The Baduy people are divided into two groups: the Baduy Dalam (Inner Baduy) and the Baduy Luar (Outer Baduy).

The Baduy Dalam is the more isolated of the two groups and is known for its strict adherence to traditional beliefs and practices, including a ban on modern technology and clothing. The Baduy Dalam also maintains a unique language and social structure, which are kept separate from the outside world.

The Baduy Luar, on the other hand, has more contact with the outside world and has adopted some modern practices. However, they still maintain many traditional customs, such as using traditional medicine and adhering to a strict moral code.

The Baduy people are also known for their unique woven cloth called "tenun Baduy" and their craftsmanship in making traditional wooden crafts.

The Baduy community places a strong emphasis on maintaining their cultural identity and preserving their way of life. As such, visitors are required to obtain permission from the community leaders and are expected to respect their customs and traditions while visiting.
